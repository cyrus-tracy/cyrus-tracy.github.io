Biostatistics and Public Health Coursework:

Introduction to Biostatistics (BIOS500H)

An introductory course to concepts in biostatistics including sampling, descriptive statistics, confidence intervals, tests of hypotheses, chi-square distribution, 2-way tables, power, sample size, ANOVA, and non-parametric tests.

Introduction to SAS (BIOS511)

An introductory class to statistical analysis software SAS. Use of SAS for data management, including an understanding of SAS documentation, ability to write moderately complex SAS programs with no outside resources besides documentation.

Foundations of Public Health (SPHG351)

An overarching introduction to public health including how to identify and describe a variety of public health issues. Also covers underlying science of disease and promoting human health, as well as the social, economic, biological, and other factors that both impact and are impacted by public health.

Mathematics Coursework:

Note: Courses at CCD indicated by *

Advanced Calculus (MATH521)

Introductory undergraduate course in real analysis. Includes the real numbers, continuity and differentiability of functions of one variable, infinite series, integration.

Calculus Series (AP Calc AB and BC, Calc 3*)

Essential calculus including calculus of one and multiple variables. Includes limits, derivatives, integration, Taylor series, vectors, double and triple integration, partial differentiation, analytic geometry.

Linear Algebra (MATH347)

An introduction to linear algebra and matrices including determinants, solution of linear systems, Gram-Schmidt procedure, and eigenvectors/ eigenvalues.

Differential Equations*

Introduction to differential equations: first and second order differential equations, series solutions, Laplace transforms, linear algebra, eigenvalues, first order systems of equations, and numerical techniques for solving differential equations

Discrete Mathematics (MATH381)

Introduction to logic, proofs, and theoretical Mathematics. Includes logic, set theory, relations and functions, induction, and permutations and combinations.
